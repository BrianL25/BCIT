Taipei - Where Cultures Collide

Taipei is a bustling city whose uniqueness lies in its identity as a cultural powerhouse. A melting pot of traditional Chinese culture blended with Japanese, Southeast Asian and American influences, Taipei is like a living museum with endless places to explore.

Walking through the streets of Taipei is like going on a historical journey. The numerous Taoist temples in the city showcases the importance of tradition and prayer in Taiwanese culture. The restored mansions built during the Japanese colonial era gives visitors a glimpse of Taiwan's vibrant yet melancholic past. Priceless treasures on display in the National Palace Museum takes visitors on a journey through 5000 years of Chinese civilization. From the beautiful merchant villas and shopfronts to memorials and cemeteries for victims of past atrocities, Taipei is a city that takes great pride in celebrating its history, both the glorious and the grievous.

Tokyo - Where the Past and the Future Coexist in Harmony

Tokyo mesmorizes visitors with its traditional culture and futuristic cityscape. From the neon-lit streets of Shinjuku, imposing skyscrapers of Ginza, and the world's busiest intersection at Shibuya to the historic Sensoji Temple and the Meiji-jingu Shrine, Tokyo is a modern city built on old patterns.

From the surface, Tokyo may seem modern or even futuristic. Nevertheless, the city possesses a rich history which can be traced all the way back to the beginning of the Edo Period (1603). In the shadows of skyscrapers, one can find small wooden bars and restaurants in quiet alleys. Between the busy intersections, one can find solace in ancient temples and shrines. In older neighborhoods, one can find traditional handmade crafts that have been produced for centuries or take a stroll down the narrow cobblestone streets where samurais and geishas once trod.
